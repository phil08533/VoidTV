DMCA Notice & Takedown Policy

Digital Millennium Copyright Act Compliance — VoidTV

VoidTV is committed to respecting intellectual property rights. This site only displays content that has been automatically verified as public domain via a multi-signal confidence scoring system requiring a minimum 90% confidence score. No video files are hosted on this server — all content is embedded directly from the Internet Archive.

How Content Is Verified

Every film displayed on VoidTV is processed by an automated public-domain verification system that checks the following signals before acceptance:

  • Release year on or before 1928 (US copyright law — automatic public domain)
  • Explicit public domain licence URL in Internet Archive metadata
  • Membership in trusted archive collections (Prelinger Archives, Silent Films, etc.)
  • Metadata subject tags indicating public domain status
  • Creator identified as a US Government agency (government works are public domain)

Films scoring below 90% confidence are automatically rejected and are never displayed.

Filing a DMCA Takedown Notice

If you are a copyright owner (or authorised agent) and believe that content displayed on VoidTV infringes your copyright, you may submit a written DMCA notice. Your notice must include all of the following:

  1. Identification of the copyrighted work — describe the work you believe has been infringed (title, registration number if applicable).
  2. Identification of the allegedly infringing content — provide the exact URL on VoidTV where the content appears.
  3. Your contact information — name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address.
  4. Statement of good faith belief — a statement that you have a good faith belief that the use of the material is not authorised by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
  5. Statement under penalty of perjury — a statement, made under penalty of perjury, that the information in your notice is accurate and that you are the copyright owner or are authorised to act on behalf of the copyright owner.
  6. Signature — your physical or electronic signature.

Notices that do not comply with all requirements of 17 U.S.C. § 512(c)(3) may not receive a response.

Send DMCA notices to:

dmca@voidtv.com

Upon receiving a valid and complete DMCA notice we will promptly investigate and, where appropriate, remove or disable access to the identified content.

Counter-Notice Procedure

If you believe that content was removed from VoidTV as a result of a mistake or misidentification, you may submit a counter-notice. Your counter-notice must include:

  1. Your physical or electronic signature.
  2. Identification of the material that was removed and the location where it appeared.
  3. A statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good faith belief that the material was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification.
  4. Your name, address, telephone number, and a statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the federal district court for your district.

Counter-notices should be sent to the same contact address above.

Repeat Infringer Policy

In appropriate circumstances, VoidTV will disable access to content associated with repeat infringers in accordance with 17 U.S.C. § 512(i).

Disclaimer

VoidTV does not host, store, or distribute any video files. All media is embedded from the Internet Archive (archive.org), which maintains its own DMCA compliance programme. Takedown requests relating to the underlying files should also be directed to the Internet Archive.
